# Healthcare Operations & Revenue Optimization Dashboard

## Overview
This project is a healthcare operations and revenue analytics solution developed in Power BI to monitor hospital performance, optimize operational efficiency, evaluate insurance revenue contribution, and analyze patient admission patterns and healthcare risks.

The dashboard transforms healthcare operational and financial data into actionable insights that support data-driven hospital management, revenue optimization, patient flow monitoring, and predictive healthcare intelligence.

The analysis covers operational efficiency, patient admissions, revenue performance, insurance provider analysis, emergency trends, medical conditions, and predictive risk indicators from January 2020 to December 2022.

---

# Business Problem
Healthcare organizations often face challenges such as:

- Inefficient patient flow management
- Rising operational and treatment costs
- Limited visibility into hospital revenue drivers
- Poor understanding of admission trends
- Difficulty optimizing bed utilization
- Limited insight into insurance provider performance
- Challenges identifying high-cost patient groups
- Delayed operational decision-making

This project was designed to provide a centralized healthcare intelligence dashboard that enables better operational planning, revenue optimization, risk monitoring, and hospital performance management.

# Objectives
The dashboard was developed to:

- Monitor hospital operational performance

# Dashboard Features

## Executive Overview
Provides a high-level summary of healthcare operational performance including:

- Total Revenue
- Average Length of Stay (ALOS)
- Emergency Admission Percentage
- Bed Turnover Rate
- Revenue per Patient

# Key Insights

## Operational Insights
- Total admissions exceeded 25,000 patients
- Average Length of Stay remained stable at 15 days
- Emergency admissions accounted for 36% of total admissions
- Bed turnover rate exceeded 64%

## Revenue Insights
- Total healthcare revenue exceeded 846.8M
- Medicare generated the highest revenue contribution
- Patients aged 66+ generated the highest revenue share
- Revenue performance varied across admission types

## Risk & Cost Insights
- High-cost admissions exceeded 2,900 cases
- Hypertension and Asthma recorded notable high-cost percentages
- Emergency admission rates fluctuated across months
- High-cost risk was more concentrated among older age groups
